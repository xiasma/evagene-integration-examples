# Cascade screening invitation

Dear Sarah Ward,

You are receiving this letter because a close relative of yours, Helen Ward, has
recently had a genetic result identified in the family. Because this result may
be shared with other relatives, your genetic counselling service has invited you
to consider what is known as **cascade screening** — a conversation and, if you
choose, a blood test that looks specifically for the variant that has been
identified in your family.

As a **Sister** of the person who was tested, you fall within the group that the
clinical team would like to reach out to. The diseases being discussed with the
family are: Breast cancer, Ovarian cancer. Whether or not to proceed is entirely
your decision; the purpose of this letter is simply to make sure you have the
opportunity to have that conversation.

You may wish to consider speaking with your genetic counsellor about:

- what the identified variant means for you personally;
- what tests, if any, are appropriate in your own case;
- what surveillance or preventative options would then be available;
- and what — if anything — this information means for your own children or
  siblings.

There is no obligation to take any action, and you are welcome to take as much
time as you need. If you would like to book an appointment, please contact the
genetics service on the number provided at the top of this letter.

Yours sincerely,

The Clinical Genetics Team
